Ladakh with Tashi

I grew up in these mountains, and I still live here. For years now I've been showing people the Ladakh I actually know — not the postcard version, the real one. Some come for the passes and lakes. Most leave talking about something else entirely.

A few things about me

My name's Tashi. Ladakhi, born and raised — Leh is home, not a destination. I learned these trails the way everyone here does, on foot and mostly by accident, long before I knew people would pay to see them.

These days I guide small groups and solo travelers through the routes I know best, and build custom trips for the ones who want something I haven't done yet. I speak Ladakhi, Hindi, and enough English to argue about which pass has the better view. (It's Wari La. Fight me.)

"If it's not on this list, tell me what you're after — we'll find a way there."

On foot, for days

Not everything I do is by jeep. Some of the best days are on foot — and some of the best trips take several of them.

These hikes need real preparation — fitness, the right gear, and time to plan. We carry our own supplies into mountains that don't have shops or roads. Message me early if this is what you're after, and we'll figure out what's possible.

Come in June and you get green valleys. Come in January and you get a Ladakh almost nobody sees.

From our kitchen, too

My family keeps a small plot outside Leh, and between trips I help out there too. We dry apricots in the sun the way my grandmother did, grow herbs for tea, and put up marmalades and flours through the season — all organic, none of it sold on this site. If you're curious what's in season when you visit, ask — I usually have some to share.
